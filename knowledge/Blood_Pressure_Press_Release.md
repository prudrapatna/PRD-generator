# Press Release: Blood Pressure Trends

**Google Launches Blood Pressure Trends for Pixel Watch and Fitbit to Help Millions Identify Invisible Cardiovascular Risks**

MOUNTAIN VIEW, CA, March 4, 2026 —

Today, Google announced Blood Pressure Trends, a first-of-its-kind, calibration-free wellness feature for Pixel Watch and Fitbit devices. Working quietly in the background, this new feature helps users discover long-term blood pressure patterns, transforming the wearable they already use every day into a proactive tool for cardiovascular awareness and lifestyle management.

High blood pressure is the world's leading risk factor for cardiovascular death, yet it is fundamentally asymptomatic—earning it the name the "silent killer." According to clinical research, approximately 50% of American adults have hypertension, and shockingly, 70% of younger adults (aged 18-44) with uncontrolled hypertension are completely unaware of their condition. Traditional monitoring requires manual cuffs and active, frequent checking, creating friction that leaves millions of people flying blind until a major health event occurs.

Blood Pressure Trends addresses this awareness gap through passive, opportunistic sensing. Powered by WavesFM, Google’s advanced multimodal AI foundation model, the feature analyzes pulse shape data from the wrist to estimate blood pressure patterns during both daily activity and sleep. Instead of reactive, single-point readings that can cause "alarm fatigue," the system aggregates 14 valid days of data over a calendar month. If sustained out-of-range patterns are detected, users receive a calm, context-rich notification directly on their device.

"People shouldn't have to wait for a health scare to learn about their cardiovascular wellness," said Dr. Karen DeSalvo, Chief Health Officer at Google. "With Blood Pressure Trends, we're removing the friction of manual cuffs. By making this invisible signal visible, we're empowering users with the longitudinal intelligence they need to make healthier daily choices and have more informed conversations with their doctors."

Beyond simple pattern identification, Blood Pressure Trends acts as a Personal Health Agent by correlating these shifts with the user's daily habits. Notifications include actionable context, connecting out-of-range patterns to recent sleep duration or activity levels. Validated against the clinical gold-standard of 24-hour Ambulatory Blood Pressure Monitoring (ABPM) in comprehensive trials, the feature achieved over 92% specificity. Blood Pressure Trends will roll out in over 50 countries globally, including the US, Canada, Japan, and across Europe.
